# Academic Revision Examples

Use this file as a compact example bank for academic writing polishing. The goal is not to memorize fixed phrasings, but to see the kind of transformation the skill should produce.

## 1. Cut clutter

Before:
> It is important to note that there are several factors that are involved in the regulation of cell growth.

After:
> Several factors regulate cell growth.

## 2. Replace weak verbs

Before:
> The results are suggestive of a reduction in symptoms after treatment.

After:
> The results suggest that symptoms declined after treatment.

## 3. Undo nominalization

Before:
> The implementation of the intervention led to an improvement in adherence.

After:
> Implementing the intervention improved adherence.

## 4. Fix paragraph logic

Before:
> Sleep duration was shorter in residents. Burnout rates were also higher. The survey was distributed electronically. Previous studies have reported mixed findings. Residents also worked more night shifts.

After:
> Residents slept less, worked more night shifts, and had higher burnout rates than attending physicians. These findings align with some prior studies, although the literature remains mixed. Because the survey was distributed electronically, nonresponse bias remains possible.

## 5. Results should summarize, not read the table

Before:
> Table 1 shows that 38% of participants in group A improved, compared with 19% in group B.

After:
> Improvement was twice as common in group A as in group B (Table 1).

## 6. Discussion should open with the answer

Before:
> This study has several limitations. Further research is needed. We found that treatment adherence improved.

After:
> We found that the intervention improved treatment adherence. Several limitations should qualify the strength and generalizability of that conclusion.

## 7. AI-sounding phrasing reduction

Before:
> These findings underscore the critical importance of a holistic and multifaceted approach to patient-centered optimization.

After:
> These findings suggest that improving care will require attention to several patient-level factors.

## 8. Preserve claim strength

Before:
> Obesity was associated with higher cancer risk.

Unsafe rewrite:
> Obesity increased cancer risk.

Safe rewrite:
> Obesity was associated with a higher risk of cancer.

## 9. Reviewer response example

Reviewer comment:
> The Discussion overstates the causal implications of the findings.

Response:
> We agree and have revised the Discussion to avoid causal language. We now state that obesity was associated with higher cancer risk, rather than claiming that obesity increased cancer risk.

## 10. Discussion paragraph example

Before:
> We found that obesity was associated with increased risk of several cancers. This meta-analysis is subject to a number of limitations. There were some inconsistencies in previous studies. It is important to note that the explanation for these findings is not clear. Further research is needed.

After:
> We found that obesity was associated with an increased risk of several cancers. However, this meta-analysis has several limitations, and prior studies have not been fully consistent. The mechanisms underlying these associations remain unclear, and further research is needed to clarify them.
